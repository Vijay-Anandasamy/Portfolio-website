Overview:

Zoho Flow is where I started working in Product Marketing and Partnerships Operations, with an initial focus on bringing in new partnerships from third-party platforms.

Over time, my contribution extended beyond partnership acquisition. I worked on creating integration-related marketing content, generating use cases based on customer needs, and coordinating with teams to support integration launches from start to go-live.

My role expanded across partnerships, product, marketing, and internal systems — not as separate functions, but as connected parts of the same workflow.

What started as working on integrations evolved into understanding how systems are built, how products scale, and how execution needs to be structured across teams.

The Challenge
Scaling without structure
  • At Zoho Flow, partnerships and integrations grew fast—but systems didn't.
  • Each integration required coordination across teams, yet there was no clear workflow. This led to delays, repeated effort, and unclear ownership.
  • Content and documentation had to be created for every app, but without structure, it became inconsistent and hard to maintain at scale.
  • As growth increased, operations became heavier—not smoother.
Where I Stepped In:
  • At Zoho Flow, I focused on building systems that could support growth without increasing operational load.
  • I introduced a centralized tracking system to monitor all live integrations and their stages across the partnership lifecycle—bringing visibility and reducing coordination gaps.
  • To address documentation delays, I built an AI-driven system that automatically generates help documentation when an integration goes live, reducing turnaround time from weeks to days.
  • Built a support-driven system that leverages customer query data from existing tickets to identify recurring problems and convert them into valuable painpoint-driven content assets for marketing team
Reflection
  • At Zoho Flow, I realized that growth without structure breaks fast.
  • You can push integrations, create content, and manage partners—but without systems, everything becomes reactive.
  • My shift was simple: stop solving tasks, start fixing the system behind them. That's what makes scale sustainable.

Overview:

Zoho Concierge was where I built my foundation in consulting and business problem solving.

This wasn't just about learning products—it was about understanding how businesses actually operate. I worked on decoding business processes, mapping customer journeys, and designing workflows that could be implemented using Zoho applications.

The role required constant interaction with customers—understanding their needs, pitching solutions, and handling communication.

More importantly, this is where I developed the ability to understand any product quickly, break it down, and use it effectively in real scenarios. It became the base for everything I did later.

The Challenge
Understanding businesses without real context
  • At Zoho Concierge, the challenge was not the product—it was the ambiguity of business problems. Customers came in with partial clarity, often describing symptoms instead of actual problems.
  • There was no structured playbook for most use cases. Each interaction required quickly understanding the business model, identifying gaps, and mapping it to the right combination of applications—all in real time.
  • Balancing learning, customer interaction, and solutioning created constant pressure.
  • The challenge was not just to suggest a solution—but to understand the business well enough to suggest the right one.
Where I Stepped In:
  • I worked on understanding business processes, mapping customer journeys, and designing workflows that aligned with how the business operates.
  • My approach towards consulting was never about suggestion, but optimization—going a step further and imagining scenarios beyond what was asked.
  • Email communication post-call became a crucial factor in converting leads, always including workflow diagrams and theoretical explanations.
  • This is also where I developed the skill of cross-selling—encouraging leads to think in terms of ecosystems rather than individual products.
Reflection
  • At Zoho Concierge, I learned that products are easy to learn—business is not.
  • The real skill lies in understanding workflows, not just features.
  • Once you understand how a business works, you can quickly pick up any product and use it effectively.
